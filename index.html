Abstract

Video anomaly detection aims to identify abnormal events in video surveillance, which is an important task in computer vision. Motivated by the power of transformer in image and video, we propose a novel architecture for future frame prediction in video anomaly detection. In order to capture long-range dependencies, we applied a transformer as an encoder to extract multi-scale feature maps of input frames. In addition, we proposed a hierarchical spatio-temporal transformer to capture the spatial and temporal features corresponding with four stages of the encoder. The extracted spatial and temporal features from the proposed transformer are merged with a convolutional neural network-based decoder via skip connection at multiple layers to predict the future frame. During the testing stage, the appearance and motion of abnormal video frames are predicted with high errors, which is used to determine whether a normal or abnormal event. Extensive experiments including various ablation studies prove that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on three benchmark datasets, including UCSD Ped2, CUHK Avenue and ShanghaiTech.
